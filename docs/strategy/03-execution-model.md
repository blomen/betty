# Execution Model

Step-by-step process from bias to entry to exit.

## Pre-Trade Process (in order)

```
1. Build narrative/bias     → 15-min chart, previous day structure
2. Identify points of interest → Volume profile levels (HVN within supply/demand)
3. Wait for price to arrive  → Patience — don't chase
4. Confirm with orderflow    → Aggression, delta, absorption patterns
5. Execute                   → 15-second / 1-min / range bars
6. Trail stop-loss           → Based on orderflow, not arbitrary ticks
```

## Entry Checklist (4-6 boxes)

| # | Criterion | How to check |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Bias/Direction** | 15-min structure — bullish or bearish (AMT state) |
| 2 | **Point of Interest** | Volume-refined level within demand/supply zone (specific HVN, not just price zone) |
| 3 | **Orderflow Confirmation** | Aggression at the level — volume signature + price follow-up |
| 4 | **Price Follow-through** | Wait for reaction THEN follow-through — no catching falling knives |
| 5 | **Time of Day** | Volatility present, past initial balance formation |
| 6 | **VWAP Position** | Standard deviation context — where is price relative to fair value? |

## Entry Execution

- **Do NOT use limit orders** — watch and wait for aggression
- **Fractional entries**: Accumulate at different levels with a common stop-loss
- Use 15-second charts or 20-tick range bars (range bars in choppy conditions)
- Entry is on the EXECUTION timeframe, not the analysis timeframe

## Exit / Trail Rules

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| BOS (Break of Structure) + orderflow confirms volume shift against you | Exit immediately |
| BOS but orderflow does NOT confirm (volume still in your favor) | Hold, move to breakeven |
| Big explosion candle in your direction | Trail stop below that candle |
| Trail frequency | Every 10-15 minutes depending on volatility |
| At session open | Can be as fast as 15 seconds → breakeven |

## What NOT to Do

- Don't trade the opening volatility spike (stop-losses too tight for opening chaos)
- Don't look at more than 2 days of data (previous + current)
- Don't use weekly highs/lows or Asia range
- Don't hold through consolidation hoping for breakout — if orderflow dries, exit
- Don't take reversals without a profit cushion from earlier trades
